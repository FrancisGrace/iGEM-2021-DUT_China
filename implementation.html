1 Overview

Human production activities and daily life are producing a large quantity of plastics debris accumulated in the environment and the plastic pollution possessing negative impacts on human life. Plastics can adsorb pollutants from the surrounding environment (which means higher specific surface area) and release pollutants into natural world. Among all these plastics forms, PET is one of the major plastics, in addition to polyethylene, polypropylene, polystyrene, polyvinyl chloride, and polyurethane, and its worldwide production amounted to more than 106 million tons in 2020. Synthetic plastic polymers Polyethylene terephthalate (PET) is one of the most widely used forms of synthetic plastics in several industrial fields, which has been reported spreading as particles less than 5 mm in environments and being captured from environments with low efficiencies. Furthermore, in addition to the microplastics found in the environment, PET is frequently used in household products, which is severely accumulated in the landfill and even worse, natural environments.

In China, as one of the world's top 10 plastic producers and consumers of plastic products, Chinese natural environment and city are faced with severe plastic pollution and high recycling pressure. In 2020, China produced 70.302 million tons of plastics and imported nearly 450 thousand tons. The total consumption of plastics in China in 2019 was about 90.877 million tons, of which millions of tons were used for packaging. In 2020, the total consumption of PET bottles in China reached 9.49 million tons. In terms of consumption level, the consumption of bottle-grade PET in China continues to grow from 6.33 million tons in 2016 to 9.49 million tons in 2020. In 2020, the total consumption of PET bottles in China reached 9.49 million tons. At the same time, a large number of plastics float on the surface of the ocean and suspend in various layers of seawater, which hinders the transmission of light in the seawater, affects the use of light by various organisms in the water, and interferes with their normal life activities. The wandering plastic is easily eaten by low-end food chain organisms such as mussels and zooplankton. Plastic can not be digested, but can only exist in the stomach all the time, occupying space, causing animals to get sick or even die. If microplastics with organic pollutants are eaten, the damage to these plankton will be even worse. The pollutants are released by enzymes in the organism, which undoubtedly aggravates the disease. Mussels, zooplankton and other organisms at the bottom of the food chain will be eaten by the upper foodchain species, and thus, the plastics debris particles and pollutants like PET material will enter the upper foodchain. Only 1% of the accumulated harmful substances of plastics from the bottom foodchain will become 20% in the upper layer, which will make a large number of organisms that eat plastics sick or die. And the current treatment of plastic pollution mainly adopts the method of salvage, centralization and incineration, which requires more manpower and may produce secondary pollutants like PM2.5.

Fortunately, in 2016, Japense scientists reported the discovery of the bacterium, Ideonella sakaiensis 201-F6, which develops a two-enzyme system to deconstruct PET plastic to TPA and EG molecules, which could be effectively catabolized as a carbon and energy sources for cells. Simultaneously, a specific protein, the hydrophobin protein is discovered to be capable of attaching to plastic surface via its hydrophobic part and expose its hydrophilic part to aqueous environment. This unique feature may effectively make enzymes more accessible to hydrophobic surface of PET. Therefore, inspired by nature, we designed a delicate multicomplex enzyme system, in which short peptide tags (Redesigned RIAD and RIDD derived from natural proteins) are applied to create scaffold-free modular enzymes assemblies. Here, in order to effectively degrade microplastic PET particles, we construct enzymes of IsPETase and MHETase and protein hydrophobin together in our complex system via scaffold modular part, which may reveal higher catalytic efficiency in mild temperature and avoid high possible loss of enzymes activity. Therefore, we created an innovative strategy to improve PET degradation at mild temperature via biosynthetic factories and artificially designed proteins system that do not exist in the nature.

As a substitute for degradable plastic, due to the current requirements of governments around the world for the prevention and control of plastic pollution, major enterprises need to be responsible for the plastic they may discharge into the environment. As a method to effectively reduce the environmental cost of enterprises, the market size is expected to reach 10 billion yuan.

4 Market and Competition

4.1 Market Policy

The "Opinions on Further Strengthening the Control of Plastic Pollution" was issued, and some plastic products will be banned, but as an effective way to prevent and control plastic pollution, the development of domestic related industries-recycled plastics industry may usher in opportunities.

By the end of 2020, China will take the lead in banning and restricting the production, sale and use of some plastic products in some areas and fields. By the end of 2022, the consumption of disposable plastic products will be significantly reduced and alternative products will be promoted.

4.2 Market size and market structure

As a major producer of plastic products, with the basic clearance of small, scattered and polluted enterprises, the growth of downstream demand has accelerated, resulting in a rapid increase in the output of plastic products. It is estimated that in 2019, the output of plastic products enterprises above designated size in China will be 79.915 million tons, up 32.26% year on year. At the same time, plastic pollution is increasing. Today, nearly 300 million tons of plastics are produced every year. About a third are thrown away immediately after use. Most waste plastics are incinerated in landfills and are likely to have residues. And most of the rest ends up in the sea. "With all the plastic produced so far, there is enough to wrap the entire planet around," said Jan Zalasvich, a scientist at the University of Leicester in the UK.

In order to strengthen the control of plastic pollution, besides restricting the use of plastic products, it is also an effective way to strengthen the recycling of waste plastics. At present, both at home and abroad are taking action. At a meeting held in Geneva, Switzerland, in early May 2019, an amendment to the Basel Convention was formulated to add dirty plastic waste to the import and export restrictions. This is the first international legal restriction on plastic waste. The Amendment Agreement shall become effective on January 1, 2021. Domestically, China has completely banned the import of waste plastics. Establish and improve the recycling system of waste agricultural film; Standardize the recycling and disposal of waste fishing nets and fishing gear. To promote the standardization, centralization and industrialization of plastic waste recycling, related projects should be gathered in parks such as resource recycling bases. And that utilization level of plastic waste resource is improved. As a fast and effective means of plastic treatment, the current market has not been completely stable. As a parallel market with degradable plastics, we believe that our enzyme preparation will also have a future market scale of nearly 10 billion yuan.

At the same time, as a special industry, the market structure of plastic treatment in China is: local government + large enterprises + scientific research institutes. Local governments put forward emission reduction requirements for large and medium-sized enterprises, focusing on prevention, and scientific research institutes provide technology for large enterprises to reduce emissions.

4.3 Competitor analysis and substitute analysis

At present, researchers in related professional fields are actively developing biodegradable technologies. In recent years, there have been a large number of related patents, but these methods have slow degradation rate or high environmental requirements. There is no large-scale promotion abroad. At present, few companies are engaged in the production of plastic-degrading enzymes. However, we are convinced that the complex enzymatic degradation system is the latest trend in the plastics treatment and recycling market and a valuable opportunity for key players.

At present, some manufacturers have produced microbial agents for degrading plastics, but these microorganisms may cause certain biosafety problems, and at the same time, microbial agents are not suitable for some special environments.

6 Business Model

6.1 Main activities

The business model of the complex enzyme system is mainly composed of two parts, one is to design and develop high-efficiency PET-degrading complex enzymes suitable for specific scenarios, and provide customized services for enterprises, such as sewage treatment plants, water sources, food processing plants, environmental protection research institutes, recycled plastic factories, etc.

The other is to provide basic optional products for mass customers.

The overall development model is designed to take full advantage of the team's development capabilities and the relatively easy preservation of the product.

As a highly mobile synthetic biology project, we can meet the needs of different scenarios. Therefore, for enterprise users, we can provide products such as enzyme preparations at different temperatures and with different purities according to their personalized needs and even for their needs.

For mass customers, the project team itself will develop some basic complex enzyme systems, which will be sold to the public, similar to the "basic style" of clothing, so as to achieve household scenarios, such as household water dispensers, air purifiers and other devices.

6.2 Profitability

For enterprise users, we contract with them and then develop enzymes that meet their needs in the laboratory. According to the technical difficulty, charge RMB 200,000-1,000,000/order

For the general public users, we provide them with a safe and directly added immobilized enzyme filter element layer in their water purifier, which is sold at the price of 100-500 yuan per unit.

6.3 Cost Structure

Our main costs are laboratory rent, labor costs, and material costs. According to the researchers who have been engaged in the research of complex enzymes for more than eight years, from the market price point of view, the cost of raw materials for complex enzymes is high, there are many manufacturers, and the bargaining power of the industry is low. Therefore, the market price of raw materials is relatively high, accounting for 20% of the total cost of products. The equipment functions required for the preparation of complex enzyme products are relatively basic. The cost of equipment in midstream laboratories and research institutes is relatively low, with an average price of less than 500000 per machine, accounting for less than 20% of the total cost of products.

In addition, advertising is also an important expense for us. We will publicize our project on the Internet, television and various biological journals, and organize or participate in relevant conferences and exhibitions.
